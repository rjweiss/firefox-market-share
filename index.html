Pageviews

This chart shows market share of browsers as measured by page views in an opt-in, non-representative sample of websites.

Data source: StatCounter

StatCounter gathers their data via analytics tracking code embedded in an affiliate's website. They report that their affiliate network is about 3 million sites, and that they base their numbers on approximately 15-17 billion page views per month.

They adjust their data in the following ways: they attempt to remove bots and they account for prerendered page views in their browser stats. Their data is otherwise unadulterated from the observed page view traffic data.

Unlike NetMarketShare, StatCounter does not reweight their affiliate traffic according to the country-level Internet usage. Their affiliate network is unfiltered at the country-level. They share an example of observed sample sizes per country is available for desktop and mobile.

Users

This chart shows market share of browsers as measured by estimated unique users that visit an opt-in, non-representative sample of websites.

Data source: NetMarketShare

NetMarketShare gathers their data via analytics tracking code (HitsLink and SharePost) embedded in an affiliate's website. They report that their affiliate network is about 40,000 webistes, and that they base their numbers on approximately 160 million unique visits per month.

Unlike StatCounter, NetMarketShare attempts to model estimated unique users by deindividuating visitor traffic, presumably at the IP address level. In other words, an IP address is counted only once per day if it visits an affiliate website.

Additionally, NetMarketShare reweights their data by country in order to account for differences in country-level Internet traffic. This is due to the lack of representativity of their sample; because some countries are not as well represented in their sample, and others are likely overrepresented, they chose to upweight or downweight their country-level web traffic in order to account for the lack of representativity.

Their country level Internet usage statistics are based on the CIA World Factbook, which last counted Internet Users in 2009.
